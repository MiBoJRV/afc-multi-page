Romance Scam Investigations by the Anti-Fraud Centre

At the Anti-Fraud Centre, we understand the devastating impact of romance scams on individuals and families. Our dedicated team of experts specializes in conducting thorough investigations into romance scams, empowering victims, and holding perpetrators accountable for their fraudulent activities.

Our Approach

Romance scam investigations require a delicate balance of empathy, diligence, and expertise. Our team employs a multi-faceted approach to uncovering evidence, tracing digital footprints, and gathering critical information to support legal proceedings. We collaborate closely with law enforcement agencies, cybersecurity experts, and other partners to ensure a comprehensive and coordinated effort to combat romance scams.

Scam Types We Deal With

  • Catfishing

    In this type of romance scam, the perpetrator creates a fake online identity to lure victims into romantic relationships. The scammer typically uses photos and information stolen from other people to establish a false persona and gain the trust of their victims.

  • Sweetheart Scams

    Also known as "sweetheart swindles," these scams involve perpetrators who pretend to be in romantic relationships with their victims, often professing their love and devotion. Once the victim is emotionally invested, the scammer may request money for various reasons, such as medical emergencies or travel expenses.

  • Military Romance Scams

    Scammers posing as military personnel exploit the trust and admiration associated with the armed forces to deceive victims into sending money or providing personal information. They may claim to be deployed overseas and in need of financial assistance or use fake stories of military service to manipulate victims emotionally.

  • Long-Distance Relationship Scams

    Perpetrators of long-distance relationship scams establish romantic connections with victims who live far away. They may use online dating platforms or social media to initiate contact and then request money under the guise of visiting or relocating to be with the victim.

  • Inheritance Scams

    Inheritance scams involve scammers who claim to have received a large inheritance but need financial assistance to access the funds. They may promise to share the inheritance with the victim in exchange for upfront payments or personal information.

  • Online Dating Scams

    Scammers exploit the popularity of online dating platforms to target individuals seeking romantic relationships. They often create fake profiles and initiate contact with potential victims, gradually building trust before requesting money or personal information.

  • Emergency Scams

    Also known as "crisis scams," these schemes involve scammers who fabricate emergencies or crises to solicit money from their victims. They may claim to be in urgent need of funds for medical expenses, legal fees, or other unexpected situations to manipulate victims into sending money quickly.

  • Blackmail Scams

    In blackmail romance scams, perpetrators engage in intimate or explicit conversations with their victims and then threaten to expose compromising information unless the victim pays a ransom. These scams can be particularly distressing and emotionally damaging for victims.

Our Services

Digital Forensics

We utilize advanced digital forensics techniques to analyze communication records, social media profiles, and other digital evidence to uncover the true identity of romance scammers.

Legal Assistance

We provide expert legal assistance to victims of romance scams, including guidance on reporting the scam to law enforcement, filing complaints with regulatory agencies, and pursuing civil remedies against scammers.

Background Checks

Our team conducts thorough background checks on individuals suspected of engaging in romance scams, including verification of identity, employment history, and criminal records.

Financial Analysis

We trace financial transactions associated with romance scams, including fraudulent wire transfers, cryptocurrency payments, and gift card purchases, to identify patterns and track down stolen funds.
